# Involuntary Unemployment

**Definition:** A situation where workers are willing and able to work at the current wage but cannot find employment.

## Key Distinctions
- **Frictional Unemployment:** People "between jobs" or looking for their first job.
- **Voluntary Unemployment:** People who choose not to work at the current wage (often due to trade union agreements or personal preference).
- **Involuntary Unemployment:** This is the core problem Keynes addressed. It occurs when aggregate demand is too low to employ all willing workers, even if they are willing to accept lower wages.

## Real-World Examples
- The mass unemployment of the 1930s (Great Depression).
- Recessions where business activity slows down and layoffs occur despite workers' willingness to continue.
