# Liquidity Preference

**Definition:** The desire of individuals and firms to hold their assets in the form of cash (or highly liquid assets) rather than as income-earning investments.

## Key Distinctions
- Unlike classical theory, where interest rates equilibrate savings and investment, Keynesian theory posits that interest rates are the price people demand to part with liquidity.
- Holding cash provides security and flexibility in an uncertain world.

## Real-World Motives
Keynes identified three primary reasons for holding money:
1. **Transactions Motive:** To carry out day-to-day purchases.
2. **Precautionary Motive:** As a reserve against unforeseen emergencies.
3. **Speculative Motive:** To hold cash when interest rates are expected to rise (meaning bond prices are expected to fall), allowing for future purchases at lower prices.
