# John Maynard Keynes (1883–1946)

**School of Thought:** Keynesian Economics  
**Era:** Mid-20th Century

## Core Contributions
- **Macroeconomics as a distinct field:** Pioneered the systematic study of the economy as a whole, rather than just individual markets.
- **Aggregate Demand:** Argued that total spending (demand) determines the level of employment and output.
- **Involuntary Unemployment:** Explained how an economy can be in equilibrium while many people are still unemployed.
- **Liquidity Preference:** Redefined interest rates as the cost of holding cash rather than the reward for saving.
- **Animal Spirits:** Highlighted the role of human emotion and uncertainty in economic decision-making.

## Key Works
- *The General Theory of Employment, Interest and Money* (1936)
- *A Treatise on Money* (1930)
- *The Economic Consequences of the Peace* (1919)

## Influence and Legacy
Keynes' work revolutionized economic policy in the mid-20th century, leading to the "Keynesian Consensus" and the adoption of fiscal stimulus and monetary management by many Western governments. He was also a key architect of the Bretton Woods system.

## Criticisms
- **Neglect of the Long Run:** Critics like Milton Friedman and Friedrich Hayek argued that Keynesian policies lead to inflation and ignore supply-side dynamics.
- **The Lucas Critique:** Argued that macroeconomic models must account for individual expectations, which change in response to policy.
- **Crowding Out:** Some economists argue that government spending displaces private investment by raising interest rates.
